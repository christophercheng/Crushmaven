How it Works

for singles for matchmakers
Friend Selection Select a Facebook friend - or any Facebook user - that you're attracted to. We'll send out an anonymous invite to get them signed in.
Admirer Lineup Once your 'attraction' is signed in, we present them with a carefully constructed lineup of potential admirers - you and nine other friends of theirs inserted in random order. Your friend identifies anyone they're attracted to. Similar to a police lineup, they won't know who their actual admirer is.
Match Notification If you're picked from the lineup, we notify both parties about the match. Your attraction might actually think they chose you first. If you're not picked, your identity remains protected.
1 Friend Selection Select any Facebook friend that you wish to set up i.e. 'your setup friend'. Select up to ten more friends that you think will make a good match. We'll pass your recommendations on to your setup friend. Note: Your friends can also request setups from you.
2 Setup Selection Your setup friend will view the facebook profiles of your recommendations and select the ones they're attracted to. We'll then invite the chosen recommendations to sign in, so we can determine if the attraction is mutual.
3 Match Assessment Once a recommended user is signed in, we present them with a carefully constructed lineup of potential admirers - your setup friend and nine others inserted in random order. Similar to a police lineup, the recommended user selects anyone they're attracted to.
4 Notifications If your setup friend is selected, both parties are notified. If not, your setup friend's identify is protected.As a matchmaker, you get visibility into the entire process. After all is said and done, you'll know exactly who liked whom.
